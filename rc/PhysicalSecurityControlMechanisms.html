Exercise 1 - Physical Security

Physical security is as critical as logical security. In logical security, you secure the systems, servers, and network devices using various software and tools. However, physical security uses various methods and techniques to ensure the safety of the organization and its infrastructure.

In this exercise, you will learn about the various methods and techniques used in physical security.

Bollards/Barricades

Bollards are vertical posts that create a protective boundary around a building. They are usually designed to mark boundaries and prevent vehicles from entering a specific area. They are usually fixed into the ground. They prevent any vehicle to just pass through. You can also have collapsible bollards, which can be used to add stand-off distances, protect garages, and provide hostile surveillance. However, they are specifically useful where there may be a need to permit authorized traffic.

Barricades prevent people from accessing a specific area. They can also be used for allowing vehicle access in a specific manner. For example, military areas usually have zigzag barricades, which forces the vehicles to slow down.

Both bollards and barricades serve as deterrent control, which is designed to deter attackers.

Mantraps

Tailgaiting and piggybacking is a big problem for organizations and is considered to be one of the easiest methods to enter the premises. Tailgating is a social engineering act of gaining access to an electronically locked system or restricted area by following a user who has legitimate access, intending to access vulnerable information. When the legitimate user swipes his badge or enters the passcode to enter the building, you enter through the secured door before it closes by following the user.

To prevent piggybacking and tailgating, you can implement mantrap. In a mantrap, only one person enters the mantrap, and the entry door closes. After the person authenticates himself or herself, the exit door opens. After the first person moves out of the exit door, the second person can enter the mantrap. Mantraps are installed to prevent tailgating and piggybacking.

Badges

In older times, ID badges contained the user’s photograph and some personal details, which were verified by the security guards. However, this scenario might also be continuing with some of the organizations. It is a problem to verify hundreds of users at any given time.

One of the easiest solutions was to then use ID badges, which carries a magnetic strip within the ID cards, which could either be scanned or swiped. Once the card is scanned or swiped, the user is identified.

A more recent invention is to use ID cards that carry Radio Frequency Identification (RFID), which could be detected by the proximity reader. It could detect the ID card with RFID and authenticate the user. The ID card carrying RFID simply transmits the number to the proximity reader and based on the number, the user is identified.

Alarms

An alarm is a detective control, which is used for detecting malicious activity. Alarms, also known as security alarms, are nowadays used everywhere, at home, in industries, and even on military premises. Organizations install alarms that can be triggered if malicious activities are performed, such as breaking into the building.

Let’s take an example. Your organization is housed in a four-story building. There is an alarm system at every door, which detects if anyone forcibly tries to open the door. If the door is forcibly opened, the alarm is triggered.

Signage

Signage plays a critical role in personnel safety. There may be certain areas that are accident prone or can be considered dangerous in a building. You would need to put appropriate signage so that people know that they are not supposed to be entering a particular area or room.

Signage can be a warning or simple information. For example, signage may warn people about the danger of fire.

Information signage may simply be placed outside a room, stating that it is a meeting room.

CCTV Cameras

CCTV cameras can detect unauthorized entrance, but they cannot prevent it. Usually, lock picking does not leave any external damage and therefore is hard to prove that it ever took place. A security camera can also work as a deterrent and provide proof in any legal proceedings.

Motion Detection

These cameras identify moving objects. They extract the overall boundaries of an object, and if the object moves out of the boundary, then the motion vectors are calculated. With the calculation of the motion vectors, the new position of the objects in motion is calculated. Good use of these cameras can be at front doors or even at the entrance to a server room.

Object Detection

Object detection is mainly used to detect specific objects. For example, you can put a camera on the street to figure out objects in motion. When an object is identified, a rectangle is drawn around it to make it look different from the other objects.

Industrial Camouflage

To protect a company’s data and network infrastructure, camouflage techniques can be used to disguise specific characteristics of the company’s network infrastructure. This can be achieved by creating decoy systems that are masquerading as the legitimate system. For example, when an attacker attempts to breach the network or has already gained unlawful access to the decoy system, their behavior and objectives can be monitored. This information can then be used to harden the production environment to prevent potential attacks and exploits.

Personnel

There are different personnel who play a critical role in physical security. Let’s have a look at each of these personnel and their roles.

Guards

To protect a building or a premise, you can deploy fences, bollards, and barricades. However, these are passive methods. On the other hand, guards are active entities that can question the individual entering the premises. Based on their decisions, they can judge whether a person is malicious or genuine.

In most organizations, guards are deployed at the main gate, as well as the entrance of the building. In some cases, guards not only make an entry of the visitor in their visitor logbook but also carry the responsibility of monitoring the CCTV cameras.

Robot Sentries

Robot sentries are autonomous robots who are designed to patrol a specific area. They are unmanned, which means that they function on their own. In some cases, such as borders, they may be armed. In some countries, they are deployed to guard critical infrastructure, such as a nuclear plant. Even though robot sentries are autonomous, they are still remotely controlled by humans.

Reception

Almost all organizations have receptions. One of the reception's key functions is to attend to visitors and ensure that the visitors sit in the designated areas. Only after authorization from an employee can they proceed to a specific area where the meeting needs to be held.

Several organizations also have Webcams placed on the reception to make an entry pass for the visitors. The entry pass has the visitor’s photograph and also has a place for the employee signature who attends the visitors.

Two-person Integrity/Control

Two-person integrity (TPI) is a type of Separation of Duties (SoD). In a critical task, two people are required to complete the task. For example, if there is a finance approval required, you would need two levels of approvals before the payment can be made out to an individual or a vendor. Another example that would fit well in this context is the bank locker. You would need your key, and then another key that belongs to bank personnel. Both keys are required to open the locker. The bank personnel cannot simply open the locker with a single key.

Locks

Locks are essential for any house or building to secure them from any kind of theft. Depending on the criticality and importance of the objects, you may put a specific type of lock. Let’s understand the types of locks that can be used to secure a building or premise.

Physical

A physical lock is either installed on the door or can be an individual lock. A physical lock is opened with a key. Typically, a physical lock would have three to four copies of the keys. There are several varieties of physical locks. One is a residential keyed entry lock. On the outside of the lock, you have a knob, and it contains a key entry hole. This type of lock is mostly used in houses.

The other type is the deadbolt lock, which is mostly used in commercial complexes. This type of lock has a solid metal bar that gets inserted into the wall for secure locking. As compared to the residential entry lock, the deadbolt lock provides higher security because you would have to cut the metal bar to open it. Both of these types of locks require a key to lock and unlock.

Electronic

Electronic locks use a key combination to open. You have to press a series of numbers in a particular order to open the electronic lock. An advanced version of an electronic lock is a cipher lock, which has more features. You can program it to make it work for a certain time. For example, you can program to make it work only from 8 AM to 6 PM during weekdays. The rest of the time and on weekends, the cipher lock will not work.

Biometric

Biometric locks usually have a fingerprint scanner. When you place the finger on the lock, it scans the fingerprint, and depending upon if you are a legitimate user, it authorizes your entry by unlocking the door. Some biometric locks also have a numeric keypad and manual lock, which require a key for the lock.

More sophisticated biometric locks can also include a retina scan. However, most of them have fingerprint scan capabilities.

Cable locks

Imagine a scenario where you need to put several laptops in an exhibition where visitors would like to experience your product. In the previous exhibition, two laptops were stolen. Since there are thousands of visitors daily, you want to ensure that the laptops are protected from theft. The ideal solution would be to use cable locks, which protect the laptops from any physical theft.

Most portable devices, such as laptops, have a security slot where the cable lock is placed and locked. The lock is connected to the cable, which has another end that is tied to the desk.

USB Data Blocker

A USB data blocker is a small USB device that is plugged into the USB port of a charging station. It is quite difficult to determine if a public charging station is prone to deliver malware or steal data from your phone when you plug it in. The USB data blocker prevents any kind of infection in your phone from the charging station. You need to plug in the USB data blocker into the charging station and then plug in your phone cable into the USB data blocker, which adds a layer of protection. Your phone is still charged, but the hackers cannot extract data.

Lighting

It is easy for an intruder to sneak into a building when it is dark. Lighting is a deterrent control that prevents intruders from breaking into a building. Consider a scenario if your building has security cameras, but it is dark inside the building. The cameras won’t be able to capture any clear picture. If someone breaks into the building, you will never be able to find out anything from the video recordings. More lighting means more security. The cameras have a clear view when there are proper lightings installed.

Fencing

Fences are usually installed to guard the perimeter of a building. Fences should be tall enough to prevent anyone from climbing over. To make it even more difficult for the intruder, you can also install razor wire on top of the fence.

Fences should be strong enough to be knocked down by an individual. Depending on the requirement, the fencing may be see-through. It could also be opaque, which prevents an individual from finding out what is on the other side.

Fire Suppression

A fire within a building can break out anytime, and most of the time, the reasons can only be found after you have managed to put out the fire. However, by this time, you have incurred huge losses as most of the equipment is damaged and left unusable. The fire suppression system should be placed in the most critical place of the building, which is the data center.

You can install a water sprinkler system in other places of the building, which can simply get triggered when there is a fire. However, in the server room or data center, a water sprinkler system would make things worse and can cause more damage to the equipment. The most suitable solution is to install a fire suppression system.

A fire suppression system has components that detect the fire through various stages, such as heat, smoke, and then fire. Most fire suppression systems have a built-in alarm system, which gets triggered when a fire is detected. Depending on the type of fire suppression system, it can either release the fire extinguishing substance automatically or manually.

Sensors

Sensors can be used for various purposes. They can be used to detect motion or even in the proximity reader to detect ID cards with RFID. Sensors can also be used for monitoring temperatures.

Let’s look at some of the key purposes where sensors play a critical role.

Motion Detection

Motion detection sensors work on their capability to detect the motion of objects. For example, consider that you have a room where a critical server is kept. No one is supposed to be entering the room. You have motion detection sensors installed. Now, if a moving object is detected, then an audible alarm can be triggered.

Noise Detection

Noise detection sensor has the capability of detecting various types of noises. For example, if it detects a large thump noise, it can trigger an alarm.

Proximity Reader

A proximity reader is used with ID cards or any other type of card that carries RFID. When the ID card is moved close to the proximity reader, its details are captured.

Moisture Detection

In data centers in an organization, detecting moisture is critical. For example, water condensation can cause various problems, such as corrosion, rust, and short-circuiting. On the other hand, no or missing moisture can cause electrostatic discharge. Therefore, in most of the server rooms, you will find that there are moisture detection sensors, which reports about moisture regularly.

Temperature

There is no doubt that the server rooms or data centers generate a large amount of heat. You need to have methods to measure the appropriate temperature, whether hot or cold. You need to ensure that the temperature is appropriate, which can be done with the help of temperature sensors. Temperature sensors can be of two types - contact-based and non-contact-based. Contact-based temperature sensors need to be connected with the object. On the other hand, non-contact-based temperature sensors can use convection and radiation to monitor temperature change.

Drones/UAV

A drone is an Uncrewed Aerial Vehicle (UAV). It is a small aerial vehicle that is used for various purposes, such as surveillance. It can fly with a controlled flight plan and is controlled through sensors and GPS.

A drone works with two different signals: rising and downlink signals. The drone receives rising signals. The rising signals can be in the form of radio command or GPS signals. However, these can be replaced by the lure signals that are sent by an attacker. The lure signals replace the rising signals to allow the attacker to take control of the fly drone. The attacker can then change the flying path of the drone.

The drone sends the downlink signals. The signals may contain the flight information or video transmission, both of which can be intercepted by the attacker. Video transmission is typically being transmitted in an unsecured manner that is susceptible to interception.

The downlink and rising signals are not protected or rather encrypted. Therefore, both the signals can be intercepted and allow the attacker to modify the transmission or take control of the drone.

Visitor Logs

When a visitor enters an organization’s premises, the guards should make an entry in the visitor logs. Along with the entry, the exit of the visitor should also be logged. Some of the key information that should be captured about the visitor is as follows:

  • Name of the visitor
  • Organization of the visitor
  • Entry date and time
  • Exit date and time
  • Mobile number
  • Name of the employee to whom the visitor needs to meet

Many organizations have removed the physical entry method, which was to capture the details in a register. Some organizations create a temporary visitor card that is valid for a fixed duration and also mentions the areas that the visitor is authorized to visit.

Some organizations create a small printed slip with all the required details and require the concerned employee to sign the slip before leaving the premises.

Whichever method that you use depends on your organization. However, it is highly critical that the visitor log be reviewed from time to time, perhaps daily, to ensure that all visitors have left the premises. If you are complying with a security standard, then the visitor logs are likely to be reviewed as part of the compliance audit.

Faraday Cages

A Faraday cage is a type of enclosure that blocks any type of electromagnetic interference. It is commonly used with devices, such as mobile phones, when you want to prevent them from any electronic interference. You can simply put the mobile device in the Faraday cage. Once you put the mobile into this package, it cannot be remotely accessed. In the forensics field, these packages are commonly used to prevent the mobile from being accessed and to retain mobile content.

Air Gap

Air gap is the segmentation of a network without any connectivity between the segments. An air gap doesn’t allow free flow of information even from within an organization’s LAN. Data can move low-to-high (unclassified to classified) with minimal processes, whereas high-to-low requires much more stringent procedures to ensure the protection of the data at a higher level of classification. An air gap is not 100% immune to viruses and malware attacks. Few viruses that use acoustic signaling may bridge the gap and reach a secure network.

Air gap networks are typically used with high security networks, such as military, financial organizations, industrial controls systems, and life-critical systems, such as nuclear plants.

Protected Cable Distribution

Protected cable distribution is about using a carrier that encases the cables, which in most cases, are the network cables. There are two reasons for using protected cable distribution. The first reason is that it protects the network cables from being damaged. The second reason is that it protects the network cables from any kind of cable tapping, which is used to extract the signals flowing over the cable.

Demilitarized Zone (DMZ)

A DMZ is a logically separated network from the internal network and allows the servers to be placed where it needs to be visible to the outside network. The network communication between the internal and the DMZ needs to be encrypted.

The Web server should be placed in the DMZ for public access, and the database server should be placed on the internal network. The database server should remain on the internal network so that if the Web server is compromised, the database server is still safe.

The servers in the DMZ have access to the Internet, but they do not have access to the internal network, which in turn can access the servers in the DMZ. This type of configuration is for security reasons. This ensures that even if an attacker(s) gain control of a server in DMZ, they cannot access the internal network.

Secure Areas

A secure area refers to an area that has limited access to users and other entities. Be it the network where the servers are connected or how the server racks are stacked in the data center. It could be a logical entity that stores the usernames and passwords. Let’s look at some of these secure areas.

Vault

A vault is a special folder or tool that is used for storing the user credentials and certificates. It can also store private, and personal files that you consider are confidential. You need to install a tool, such as McAfee LiveSafe, and create a vault. Once you do that, you need to unlock and put your files in it. After this, you need to lock the vault once again.

When you lock the vault, a set of vault files are created on your hard drive or a USB drive. You need to ensure that you safeguard the vault files because if they are lost, then you will lose the data inside the vault.

Safe

You may be taking backups from the server on tapes or any other media. However, to be able to safeguard the media from falling into the wrong hands, you need to put them under lock and key, which simply means using a safe. A safe can be manually operated, which means you need to have a key. Alternatively, it could also work with a digital key lock, which means you need to have the number combination to open the safe.

Whichever type of safe you use, you have to ensure that you do not let the keys or number combination fall into the wrong hands. Access to the safe should be highly restricted. Anyone with access to the safe must also make a log entry with the date and time and the purpose of using the safe.

Hot and Cold Aisle

There are usually numerous numbers of devices in a data center, ranging from routers, switches to servers. Each of these generates quite a bit of heat, especially the servers stacked up in server racks.

In a cold aisle, you need to put the fronts of the racks facing each other. The fronts create the cold aisle, whereas the backs create the hot aisle. The HVAC airflow would exhaust the hot air outside the data center and bring in fresh air to the cold aisle.

Exercise 2 - Data Destruction and Disposal Methods

When you delete data from a Windows system, it is soft delete. This means that the data is actually not deleted from the hard drive. This is because the files in Windows are tracked using the File Allocation Table (FAT) or using the Master File Table (MFT). FAT or MFT keeps track of the beginning and end of a file. When you delete a file from the hard drive, the operating system, Windows, in this case, deletes the pointer that is linked with the file. This pointer is responsible for pointing to the file. When the pointer is deleted, the space for its related file is marked available. To the user, the file is now deleted as space is shown as free. However, the actual files are not deleted and can be recovered by data recovery programs, such as Recuva. Some of the well-known data recovery programs are:

  • Recuva
  • Wise Data Recovery
  • Stellar Data Recovery
  • PC Inspector File Recovery
  • EaseUS Data Recovery Wizard Free

Data Destruction Methods

There are several data destruction and media sanitization methods. Some of them will destroy the media to ensure that the data cannot be recovered. Other methods will simply make the media unusable so that it cannot be used for data recovery.

Pulverizing

Shredding and pulverizing data devices use the same method of destruction. Physical destruction methods such as using high-powered devices are used to destroy these devices to ensure the data on these devices is not recoverable at all. Care must be taken before these devices are destroyed, ensuring that no data will be needed in the future.

Degaussing

The degaussing method uses an electromagnet to wipe off the data and make the media useless. This makes the drive completely inoperable and impossible to recover any data from it.

Drilling

The drilling method uses a hammer and nails to destroy a hard drive. You can hammer the nails in the hard drive that creates holes to prevent the drive from spinning. Using this method, you can also damage the read/write head of the hard drive to ensure that no data can be recovered.

Pulping

The pulping method is used to destroy sensitive data on papers. It can be used with shredded or non-shredded paper. The ink is separated from the paper using the flotation method, and then fiber is removed. After the water dries out, the pulp is created and then converted into recycled paper.

Shredding

Shredding is the process of destroying sensitive paper documents using a paper shredder. Paper shredders cut the paper into small sizes, which is almost impossible to put back together again. Care must be taken when disposing of these shredded documents to ensure that it does not fall into the wrong hands.

Burning

Another method for destroying sensitive paper documents is by burning these documents, which will ensure it will be totally unrecoverable. An incinerator can be used specifically for this task.
